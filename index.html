Company Overview

Founding & Vision

Founded: 2017, Hamburg, Germany

Founders:

  • Christian Toetzke - Mass participation event veteran
  • Moritz Fürste - 2x Olympic gold medalist (Hockey)
  • Michael Trautmann - Marketing expert
Mission: "Create the golf course for fitness people" - Making fitness a competitive sport

The Problem They Solved

In Germany, 52% of gym-goers call 'fitness' their main sport, but fitness wasn't actually a sport with competitive events.

The Solution

Standardized Format: 8 × 1km runs + 8 functional workout stations

Measurable Performance: Precise timing and rankings

Mass Participation: Inclusive for all fitness levels

Growth Timeline

2015-2017: Foundation

Founders identify market gap, develop standardized format, launch first race in Hamburg with 650 participants

2018-2020: European Expansion

Rapid growth across Germany, expansion to UK and other European markets. PUMA partnership begins.

2021-2022: Global Scaling

US market entry, 90,000 annual participants. Parent company shows 26% net profit margins.

2023-2024: Mainstream Adoption

100% YoY growth begins. Major brand partnerships. 300,000+ participants across 81 races.

2025-2026: Market Leadership

650,000+ participants, $140M revenue, 83 events, 10,000+ gym affiliates. Olympic bid preparations.

2027-2032: Olympic Vision

Professional circuit development, media rights expansion, Olympic inclusion campaign for Brisbane 2032.

Challenges & Risks

Operational Challenges

Scalability Constraints:
Equipment-intensive operations require significant capital investment for expansion
Venue Dependencies:
Need large indoor spaces in prime locations, subject to availability and cost inflation

Market Risks

  • Economic Sensitivity: Entry fees ($130-180) are discretionary spending
  • Retention Unknown: Long-term repeat participation rates unclear
  • Geographic Variability: Different pricing tolerance across markets

Competitive Threats

Format Replication:
Limited IP protection on race format - easy to copy core concept
Market Saturation:
Key markets (London, NYC) may reach capacity limits for premium-priced events

Strategic Risks

  • Founder Dependence: High reliance on Toetzke and Fürste for vision and credibility
  • Single Format Risk: Limited diversification compared to multi-format competitors
  • Olympic Uncertainty: IOC inclusion is not guaranteed despite aspirations
